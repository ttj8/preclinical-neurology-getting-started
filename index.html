Pre-clinical year neurology:
Getting Started

Interested in neurology? You do not need to decide yet. Start by exploring the field, meeting people, and finding the questions that pull you in.

Scroll ↓

Explore first.
Decide later.

Students arrive at neurology through many different paths: neuroscience research, engineering, psychology, neurosurgery, internal medicine, critical care, rehabilitation, or simply a memorable patient encounter.

Early involvement is not about committing to a specialty. It is about discovering what feels interesting.

Start with
one small step.

1

Show up

Attend an event hosted by the Student Interest Group in Neurology, meet residents, or sit in on Neurology Grand Rounds.

2

See the work

Visit BrainWorks, Yale’s human neuroscience research and outreach center, or shadow in clinic, on consults, or in the neuro ICU.

3

Email someone

Many mentorships begin with a simple message explaining your common interests
